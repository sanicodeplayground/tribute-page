Jacob W. Davis

Latvian who invented jeans

Here's a time line of Jacob W.Davis' life:

  • 1831 - Born in Riga, Latvia
  • 1854 - Emigrates to the United States, arriving in New York City where he changed his name to Jacob Davis.
  • 1858 - Davis leaves California and moves to Western Canada. There, gets married and works at the Fraser River panning for gold
  • January 1867 - returns to San Francisco
  • 1868 - Reno, Nevada where he opens a tailor shop in the main street of the town. The fabric Davis worked with was heavy-duty cotton duck cloth and cotton denim which he bought from Levi Strauss & Co., a dry goods company in San Francisco.[5] To strengthen the stress points of the sewn items he was making, Davis used copper rivets to reinforce the stitching
  • 1870 December - The birth of jeans
  • 1872 - approaches Levi Strauss, who was still his supplier of fabric, and asked for his financial backing in the filing of a patent application
  • May 20, 1873 - US Patent No. 139,121 for "Improvements in fastening pocket openings" was issued in the name of Jacob W. Davis and Levi Strauss and Company.
  • Davis continues to work for Strauss for the remainder of his life, overseeing production of the work pants as well as other lines including work shirts and overalls
  • 1908 - dies in San Francisco, at the age of 76-77
